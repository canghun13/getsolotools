Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between an expense report and an invoice?

An invoice is a request for payment for services rendered. An expense report documents money you've already spent on behalf of a client or project — typically seeking reimbursement. Use an invoice to bill for your time; use an expense report to bill for out-of-pocket costs like travel, software, or materials.

Which expenses can freelancers typically deduct on taxes?

Common deductible business expenses include software and tools, home office costs, business travel, professional development, equipment, phone and internet (business-use portion), and professional services like accounting fees. Keep receipts and records for everything — this expense report can serve as that record. Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.

Should I include receipts with the expense report?

For client reimbursement, most clients will want receipts attached — especially for amounts over $25. For your own tax records, you should keep receipts regardless of amount. The expense report itself is a summary; the receipts are the supporting documentation.

How often should freelancers submit expense reports?

For client reimbursements, submit at the end of the project or monthly for ongoing work — whatever your contract specifies. For tax purposes, track expenses as they happen and do a monthly review. Waiting until tax season makes it much harder to remember what each expense was for.

Is this expense report template legally valid?

This tool generates a professional expense report suitable for client reimbursement and tax record-keeping. It's not a substitute for legal or tax advice. Requirements vary by client and by tax jurisdiction — if you have specific compliance needs, consult a professional.
