Asking for a Referral Email Template

Referrals are the highest-quality leads a freelancer can get — and most clients are happy to give them if you just ask. The key is timing (right after a successful project) and tone (low-pressure, appreciative, and specific about what you're looking for).

Email Template
Subject: A Small Favor - [Your Name]
Hi [Client Name], I hope you're doing well. I really enjoyed working on [project name] with you and I'm glad it went well. I'm currently looking to take on new projects, and I wondered if you might know anyone in your network who could use help with [your service]. Even a brief introduction or mention would mean a lot. Of course, no pressure at all — I just wanted to ask. Thanks again for being such a great client to work with. Best, [Your Name]

Tips for This Email

  • Send this within 1-2 weeks of a successful project completion — while the experience is fresh.
  • Be specific about what kind of work or client you're looking for.
  • Make it easy to say no — 'no pressure' language paradoxically makes people more likely to say yes.
  • Never ask for a referral before the project is finished or if the project didn't go well.
