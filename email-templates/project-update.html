Project Status Update Email Template

Regular status updates keep clients confident and prevent micro-management. You don't need to send one every day — weekly or at key milestones is enough. The key is consistency. Clients who feel informed rarely panic.

Email Template — click to copy
Subject:Project Update: [Project Name] - [Week/Date]
Hi [Client Name], Here's a quick update on where things stand with [project name]. Completed this week: - [Task or deliverable] - [Task or deliverable] In progress: - [Task] - on track for [date] Coming up next: - [Next milestone or deliverable] by [date] No blockers at the moment. [Or: I do need [X] from you by [date] to stay on schedule.] Let me know if you have any questions. [Your Name]

Tips for This Email

  • Break updates into three sections: done, in progress, and coming next. It's scannable and clear.
  • Always flag if you need something from the client and by when. Don't let blockers hide in prose.
  • If you're behind, say so proactively. Clients handle bad news better when it's not a surprise.
  • Keep it brief — a status update email should take less than 60 seconds to read.
