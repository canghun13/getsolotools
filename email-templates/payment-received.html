Payment Received Confirmation Email Template

A payment confirmation email takes 30 seconds to write and leaves a lasting positive impression. It closes the loop professionally, gives the client a receipt for their records, and creates a natural opening for future work. Most freelancers skip it — which is exactly why doing it makes you stand out.

When to send Send this the same day you confirm the payment has cleared in your account. Don't send it the moment they say they've paid — wait until you've actually received it.

The Template

Email Template
Subject: Payment Received - Invoice #[001] - Thank You
Hi [Client Name], Just a quick note to confirm I've received your payment of $[amount] for Invoice #[001]. Thank you! The invoice is now fully settled. I've attached a receipt for your records. It was a pleasure working with you on [project name]. I hope we get the chance to work together again — don't hesitate to reach out when your next project comes up. Best, [Your Name]

Tips for Getting This Right

  • Attach a receipt or the invoice marked 'Paid' — clients need this for their own bookkeeping and tax records.
  • Mention the project name to help them file the receipt correctly.
  • Include a soft invite to work together again — it plants the seed without being pushy.
  • Keep it short. This email is a courtesy, not a conversation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Sending before the payment has actually cleared — if it bounces or reverses, you'll need to awkwardly follow up again.
  • Skipping this email entirely — it's a missed opportunity to close the relationship professionally and open the door to repeat business.
  • Making it too salesy — 'Hope we can work together again!' is fine; a paragraph pitching your next service is not.

Variations

For a partial/deposit payment

Hi [Client Name], Confirming I've received the deposit of $[amount] for [project name]. Thank you! This covers the first payment as agreed. The remaining balance of $[amount] will be due on [date]. I'll be in touch once I get started. Looking forward to working with you. [Your Name]

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I send a receipt or just this email?

Both. The email confirms receipt; the attached receipt (or marked-paid invoice) is the formal record the client needs for accounting. Use your invoice tool to generate a receipt, or simply re-send the invoice marked 'Paid.'

What's the difference between an invoice and a receipt?

An invoice is a request for payment. A receipt confirms payment was received. Clients need the receipt for their expense records — always provide one.

Do I need to keep a copy of this email?

Yes. Keep records of all payment confirmations for at least 3-7 years for tax purposes. Most email clients and invoicing tools do this automatically.
