Requesting a Deadline Extension Email Template

Asking for a deadline extension is uncomfortable, but handling it badly is worse. The key is to communicate early, give a specific new date, and be brief about the reason. Clients generally understand — what frustrates them is finding out late or not at all.

Email Template
Subject: Timeline Update: [Project Name]
Hi [Client Name], I wanted to get ahead of something regarding the [project name] deadline. Due to [brief, honest reason - e.g. unexpected complexity in X / a personal situation / client delays on Y], I need to request a short extension on [deliverable] from [original date] to [new proposed date]. I'm committed to delivering excellent work on this project, and I'd rather flag this now than rush the output. Everything else in the project timeline remains on track. Please let me know if this works for you, or if you'd like to discuss alternatives. Best, [Your Name]

Tips for This Email

  • Ask as early as possible — the longer you wait, the harder it is for the client to accommodate.
  • Give a specific new date, not 'a few more days.' Vagueness signals disorganization.
  • Keep the reason brief and honest. One clear sentence is enough.
  • Confirm that the rest of the project is on track to reassure them this is an isolated issue.
  • Offer to get on a call if the deadline is particularly critical for them.
