Requesting a Testimonial Email Template

Testimonials are one of the most powerful tools for winning new clients — but almost no freelancer thinks to ask for them. The trick is to ask at exactly the right moment (right after a win) and make it as easy as possible to say yes.

Email Template — click to copy
Subject:Quick Favor: Testimonial for [Your Name/Business]
Hi [Client Name], I hope you're well. I really enjoyed working on [project name] with you and I'm thrilled with how it turned out. I'm putting together some testimonials for my portfolio / website, and I'd love to include your feedback. If you have 5 minutes, it would mean a lot to me. A few sentences is plenty. Anything you'd be comfortable sharing about working together — the process, the results, or the experience — would be wonderful. You can reply directly to this email or [leave a review on LinkedIn / Google / etc.]. Thank you so much — I really appreciate it. Best, [Your Name]

Tips for This Email

  • Ask within 1-2 weeks of project completion — enthusiasm fades quickly.
  • Tell them what format you need: a reply to the email, a LinkedIn recommendation, or a Google review.
  • If they struggle to know what to say, offer 2-3 guiding questions: What was the challenge? What was the result? Would they recommend you?
  • Never ask for a testimonial if the project had issues — wait for a genuinely happy client.
