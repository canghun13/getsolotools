Late Payment Notice Email Template

If your friendly reminder went unanswered, it's time to be more direct. This email is firmer in tone but still professional. It references the late fee clause in your contract (if you have one) and asks for a response — not just payment.

Email Template
Subject: Overdue Notice: Invoice #[001] - [X] Days Past Due
Hi [Client Name], I'm writing regarding Invoice #[001] for $[amount], which was due on [due date] and is now [X] days overdue. I've not yet received payment or a response to my previous follow-up. Please let me know if there's an issue with the invoice or if there's a delay I should be aware of. Per our agreement, a late fee of [X]% may apply to invoices outstanding beyond [X] days. Please process payment as soon as possible, or contact me to discuss if there's a problem. [Payment details or link] Regards, [Your Name]

Tips for This Email

  • Reference the exact number of days overdue and the original due date.
  • Mention your late fee policy here if your contract includes one — it creates urgency without threats.
  • Ask them to respond even if they can't pay immediately. Communication is better than silence.
  • Keep the tone firm but not hostile — you may still want to work with this client again.
  • Send this 7-14 days after your first follow-up if there's been no response.
