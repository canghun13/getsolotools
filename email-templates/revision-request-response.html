Responding to a Revision Request Email Template

Revision requests are normal — but scope creep is where freelance projects break down. This template lets you respond graciously to in-scope revisions while clearly flagging anything extra, along with the cost and timeline impact.

Email Template
Subject: Re: Revisions on [Project Name]
Hi [Client Name], Thanks for the feedback on [deliverable name] — I appreciate the detail. I can make the following revisions within our agreed scope: - [Change 1] - [Change 2] Regarding [out-of-scope request], this falls outside the original scope we agreed on. I'm happy to include it as an additional item — the estimated cost would be $[amount] and it would add [X] days to the timeline. Let me know how you'd like to proceed, and I'll get the in-scope revisions started right away. Best, [Your Name]

Tips for This Email

  • Always confirm what's in scope before flagging what isn't — it softens the message.
  • Give a specific price and timeline for out-of-scope work, not a vague 'we can discuss it.'
  • Don't apologize for enforcing your scope — you're running a business, not a charity.
  • If scope creep is recurring, revisit your contract language for future projects.
