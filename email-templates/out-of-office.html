Freelance Out of Office / Vacation Notice Email Template

Freelancers often feel guilty taking time off — but communicating clearly about your absence is both professional and reassuring to clients. A proactive heads-up sent before your vacation is far better than clients wondering why you've gone quiet. Most clients appreciate the notice and will plan around it.

When to send Send this 1-2 weeks before your planned absence — earlier for longer trips or if the timing affects active project deadlines. Don't send it the day before you leave.

The Template

Email Template
Subject: I'm Out of Office [Dates] — Here's What to Expect
Hi [Client Name], I wanted to give you a heads up that I'll be away from [start date] to [end date] and will have limited access to email during this time. Before I go: - I'll complete [any outstanding deliverables] by [date before departure] - Anything that comes in while I'm away will be addressed when I return on [return date] [Optional: For urgent matters, you can reach me at [contact] / I'll be checking email briefly on [specific day].] Let me know if there's anything you'd like me to prioritise before I leave. I'll be in touch when I'm back. Best, [Your Name]

Tips for Getting This Right

  • Send this proactively — don't wait for a client to reach out while you're gone and find out then.
  • List any deliverables you'll complete before leaving so clients know what to expect.
  • Give a clear return date so clients can plan accordingly.
  • If you'll have any access to email during the break, say so — but only if it's genuine. Don't promise availability you won't maintain.
  • Confirm the return date twice — once when you send the notice, and once when you return.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not telling clients until after you've left — this creates anxiety and damages trust.
  • Promising email access you won't actually maintain — it's better to set low expectations and exceed them than to promise availability you can't deliver.
  • Leaving active deliverables unfinished before departure — complete or clearly hand off anything time-sensitive before you go.
  • Forgetting to set an auto-reply on your email — even if clients already know, a reminder auto-reply is a professional backup.

Variations

Auto-reply version

Thanks for your email. I'm currently out of office from [date] to [date] and will have limited access to messages during this time. I'll respond to all emails when I return on [return date]. For urgent matters: [contact or instructions, if applicable]. — [Your Name]

Frequently Asked Questions

How much notice should I give clients before going on vacation?

1-2 weeks is standard for most absences. For longer trips (2+ weeks) or if the timing affects an active project, give 3-4 weeks notice. The more a client depends on your regular availability, the more notice they need.

Do I need to be available while on vacation?

No — and you shouldn't promise availability you won't maintain. Set clear expectations, complete your outstanding work before you leave, and enjoy your time off. Clients who respect you as a professional will respect your time off.

What if a client has an emergency while I'm away?

If you've set clear expectations upfront, this is largely on them. You can optionally provide an emergency contact if it's genuinely urgent (and if you want to), but you're not obligated to be available on vacation.

Should I set an email auto-reply?

Yes, always. Even if clients already know you're away, an auto-reply serves as a professional reminder and sets the right expectation for anyone who didn't know. Keep it brief and include your return date.
