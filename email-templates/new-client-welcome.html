New Client Welcome Email Template

First impressions matter. A welcome email sent right after a client says yes sets the tone for the whole project. It shows you're organized, confirms next steps, and prevents the confusion that comes from jumping straight into work without alignment.

Email Template
Subject: Welcome - Next Steps for [Project Name]
Hi [Client Name], I'm thrilled to be working with you on [project name]. I wanted to send a quick welcome note and outline what happens next. Here's what to expect: 1. Contract: I'll send the project contract shortly for your review and signature. 2. Intake: I may send a short questionnaire to gather the details I need to get started. 3. Kickoff: Once the contract is signed and the deposit is received, we'll [schedule a kickoff call / I'll get started on [date]]. Project timeline: [start date] to [end date] Payment terms: [deposit amount] upfront, remainder due [upon completion / in milestones] If you have any questions before we get started, don't hesitate to reach out. I'm looking forward to this project. Best, [Your Name]

Tips for This Email

  • Send this within 24 hours of the client confirming they want to proceed.
  • Number your next steps so the client knows exactly what to expect and in what order.
  • Confirm the payment terms here — don't leave them ambiguous until the invoice arrives.
  • If you're requiring a deposit, state it clearly and early.
  • Keep the tone warm but structured — you're setting a professional standard from the start.
