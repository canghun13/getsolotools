Late Fee Laws for Freelancers in Missouri

Missouri's 9% Default Rate

Under Mo. Rev. Stat. § 408.020, creditors — including freelancers — are entitled to 9% annual interest on written contracts and accounts once payment is due and demand has been made, whenever the parties haven't agreed to a different rate. That 9% default is meaningfully higher than the 5–8% defaults used in several neighboring states, which gives Missouri freelancers a stronger fallback if they forget to write a specific rate into a contract. Most freelancers still write their own rate — typically 1.5% per month (18% annually) — directly into contracts and invoices, since it's roughly double the statutory default and Missouri courts will enforce it as the agreed rate.

Illinois' statutory default rate is lower, but its Small Claims Court jurisdiction reaches twice as high, and Illinois freelancers get an added layer of protection through the Illinois Freelance Worker Protection Act's triple-damages provision. Missouri has no equivalent freelance-specific statute, so a written contract with a clear late fee clause does more of the work — and for balances over $5,000, Missouri freelancers need the regular Associate Circuit Court civil docket rather than small claims.

Setting Up Enforceable Late Fees in Missouri

  • Write your rate directly into the contract. "Invoices unpaid after 15 days accrue interest at 1.5% per month" both beats the 9% default and starts the clock on your own schedule.
  • Repeat the rate on every invoice so it's visible to the client without needing to reference the original agreement.
  • Keep it proportional. 1.5–2% per month is standard nationally and consistent with what Missouri courts expect from a reasonable late fee rather than a punitive one.
  • Know your filing window. Missouri gives you 5 years to sue on a written contract for payment of money (RSMo § 516.120), so keep dated invoices and contracts on file.

Watch the $5,000 ceiling. If an unpaid balance (excluding interest, costs, and attorney fees) is likely to exceed $5,000, plan for the regular Associate Circuit Court civil docket from the start rather than assuming small claims will work — claim-splitting to stay under the limit is treated as an abuse of process in Missouri.

Collecting in Missouri

Missouri's Small Claims Division, part of the Associate Circuit Court, handles money disputes up to $5,000 (RSMo § 482.305) with simplified procedures — no formal discovery, and either side can appear without a lawyer, though attorneys are permitted. Filing fees are notably low, typically $35–$45 depending on county.

  • Missouri Small Claims Division: up to $5,000 (excluding interest, costs, and attorney fees)
  • Above $5,000: regular Associate Circuit Court civil docket, up to $25,000, or Circuit Court above that
  • Written contract statute of limitations: 5 years (RSMo § 516.120)
  • Post-judgment interest: prime rate plus 5% under RSMo § 408.040, roughly 12–13% in 2026

What late fee rate is enforceable in Missouri? +

Missouri's default legal rate is 9% per year when a contract doesn't specify its own rate (Mo. Rev. Stat. § 408.020). If you write a different rate into your contract and invoices — such as the common 1.5% per month (18% annually) — that agreed rate applies instead, and courts will enforce it as long as it's reasonable.

Is Missouri's small claims limit lower than neighboring states? +

Yes. Missouri's Small Claims Division caps claims at $5,000 (RSMo § 482.305), while neighboring Illinois allows up to $10,000. A Missouri freelancer owed more than $5,000 has to file in the regular Associate Circuit Court civil docket instead of the cheaper, more informal small claims process.
